A clean handoff

Keep five lanes visibly separate.

Recent care facts

Use times and units for feeds, wet/dirty diapers, sleep, pumping, and any medicine given exactly under clinician direction.

Family observations

Write what a caregiver saw, heard, measured, or recorded. Add the time and context; do not relabel an impression as a clinical conclusion.

Care-team instructions

Copy the clinician's wording for what to do, what to watch, when to call, and which routine or after-hours route to use.

Open questions

Leave uncertainty visible. The next caregiver should know what still needs an answer rather than inheriting a confident guess.

Owned follow-up

Give each call, pickup, wash, refill, observation, or appointment task a named caregiver and a due time or trigger.

Privacy choice

Share the minimum useful record through a channel the family and care team accept. Baby records can include private health and routine information.

Close the loop before walking away.

Outgoing caregiver

  • Lead with what needs attention next
  • State times instead of “recently”
  • Label observations and clinician instructions
  • Name anything unanswered
  • Confirm who owns each next step

Incoming caregiver

  • Repeat back the next action and due time
  • Ask what changed during the shift
  • Confirm routine and urgent contact routes
  • Do not infer missing medicine or care details
  • Call the care team when instructions are unclear
Medical safety note: this template is for record-keeping and caregiver communication. It cannot diagnose, assess severity, recommend treatment or dosing, or decide whether care can wait. Do not delay a pediatrician call or emergency care to finish a handoff. Follow your child's qualified care team and its routine, urgent, and after-hours routes. Contact emergency services for an emergency or any warning sign the care team has identified as urgent.
