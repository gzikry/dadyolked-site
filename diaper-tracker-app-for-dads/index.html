Useful dad job: Own the diaper record. It is one of the easiest ways to reduce the mental load, especially at pediatrician visits.
What to track

A diaper tracker should make the pediatrician conversation easier.

Wet count

Record wet diapers by time so low-output concerns do not depend on memory.

Dirty details

Note dirty diapers, color changes, texture, blood or mucus concerns, and anything your pediatrician asked you to watch.

Feeds nearby

Diapers make more sense beside feeding and sleep logs. DadYolked keeps those records together.

Safety note: Low wet diapers, signs of dehydration, fever, blood in stool, persistent diarrhea, repeated vomiting, poor feeding, unusual lethargy, or anything that feels wrong should go to your pediatrician or urgent care guidance. DadYolked is a record, not a diagnosis.
