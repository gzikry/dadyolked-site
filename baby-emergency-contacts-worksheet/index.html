Build the contact layer

Capture facts, numbers, and instructions—not a diagnosis.

Verify every number

Call or confirm the pediatrician's routine and after-hours routes. Ask what to do when the office is closed and record the answer in the care team's wording.

Make location obvious

Write the baby's full name, date of birth, home address, apartment or access details, and the caregiver's current location when it differs.

Separate local routes

Record the emergency services number for your location and the relevant poison help service. U.S. families can reach Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222.

Keep medical facts current

Copy allergies, current clinician-directed medicines, and key conditions from the current care record. Date the update and do not rely on memory.

Name nearby backup

List parents or guardians plus a trusted nearby adult who can help with another child, pets, transportation, keys, or meeting responders.

Share carefully

This sheet can contain private health, address, insurance, and contact information. Give access only to caregivers who need it and replace stale copies.

Five-minute caregiver handoff

Make the sheet usable, not merely complete.

Before the handoff

  • Verify the current location and address
  • Check that numbers and office hours are current
  • Point out allergies and clinician-directed medicines
  • Show where insurance and consent documents are kept
  • Remove old copies from bags and the fridge

Ask the caregiver to repeat back

  • Which number is the routine pediatrician line?
  • What is the care team's after-hours route?
  • What is the local emergency services number?
  • Where is the current care record?
  • Who handles transport, siblings, pets, or access?
Safety note: this worksheet is for contact storage and caregiver preparation only. It does not diagnose, triage, recommend treatment or dosing, set symptom thresholds, or decide whether care can wait. Do not delay a call or emergency care to complete the sheet. Follow the child's qualified care team and local emergency guidance. In the United States, call 911 for an emergency and Poison Help at 1-800-222-1222 for a possible poisoning; elsewhere, use the appropriate local services.
